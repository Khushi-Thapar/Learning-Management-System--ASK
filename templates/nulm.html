About DAY-NULM

RCED is working with the Municipal Corporation, Chandigarh for conducting skill development programs under the Employment Through Skill Training and Placement (EST&P) division of the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairsâ€™ Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM). The broader goal of the Program is to provide wage or self employment to the urban poor, in order to enhance their livelihood. It also aims to bridge the gap that exists between the demand for skilled labour and the supply of quality personnel by operating relevant skill development programs. The objectives of the EST&P Program include:

  • 1. To provide formal skill training to the economically weaker sections of the urban society that can help them obtain employment.
  • 2. To improve the standard of living of the beneficiaries by giving them the opportunity to increase their income with the help of targeted, marketed oriented courses.
  • 3. To ensure welfare of the urban poor through sustainability of livelihood.
  • 4. To safeguard their interests by providing them the necessary skills that can help them contribute to the economy.

RCED has trained candidates in the following trades under the Program:
  • 1. Accounts Assistant using Tally
  • 2. Retail Sales Associate
  • 3. Domestic Data Entry Operator
  • 4. Front Office Associate
  • 5. Financial Accounting and Tally

    • Apart from these courses, we offer additional compulsory inputs such as IT training as well as Soft Skills training which includes Personality Development, Spoken English and Communication Skills to fully prepare the trainees for taking up jobs.
